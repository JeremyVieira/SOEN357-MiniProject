1. User Research and Persona Creation

User Research

The user research involved secondary research using existing studies on medication management and health technology adoption. This approach was chosen to understand users' needs, challenges, and behaviors without direct user interactions, allowing insights to be gathered efficiently. The research focused on:

  • Medication management challenges
  • Appointment tracking methods
  • Communication barriers with healthcare providers
  • Technology comfort levels

To answer these questions, I will be using the following sources:

  • "Effectiveness of Mobile Health Intervention in Medication Adherence: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis". The study evaluates how effective mobile app interventions can be in enhancing medication adherence for patients with chronic diseases.
  • "User Engagement With mHealth Interventions to Promote Treatment Adherence and Self-Management in People with Chronic Health Conditions: Systematic Review". The review examines how users engaging with mobile health interventions affects their treatment adherence and self-management in individuals with chronic diseases.
  • "Involvement in Medication Safety Behaviors Among Older People with Chronic Diseases: Systematic Review of Intervention Studies". The study evaluates interventions that aim to promote involvement in medication safety in older adults with chronic diseases.
  • "Design Guidelines of Mobile Apps for Older Adults: Systematic Review and Thematic Analysis". The review gives a comprehensive guide on how to design a mobile app while keeping older people in mind.
  • "Mobile Health Applications for Older Adults: A Systematic Review of Interface and Persuasive Feature Design". The review recommends certain designs for mobile health applications aimed towards older adults. It emphasizes the importance of intuitive interfaces.

The research phase played a crucial role in shaping the design of MedTracker. By conducting secondary research through existing studies on medication management and health technology adoption, valuable insight was gathered about users' challenges and needs. This approach provided an efficient way to understand user behavior, pain points, and motivations. Key findings from the research included:

  • Medication management challenges: Users struggled with remembering complex medication schedules and coordinating appointments with multiple healthcare providers.
  • Technology comfort levels: Older adults exhibited moderate tech comfort, necessitating an intuitive and straightforward interface.
  • Communication barriers with healthcare providers: Users needed a streamlined method to share health information efficiently.

These insights directly influenced the design choices, ensuring the app was:

  • Simple and Intuitive: The design avoided unnecessary complexities, favoring a clean, user-friendly layout. This approach catered to users with varying technology comfort levels, particularly older adults who preferred straightforward navigation.
  • Colorblind Friendly: The color palette was carefully selected with high contrast and colorblind-friendly combinations, ensuring readability for all users.
  • Consistent and Familiar Patterns: Utilizing familiar UI components (e.g., buttons, links, and navigation styles) enhanced usability, allowing users to interact with the app comfortably without a steep learning curve.
  • Emphasis on Accessibility: The text size, padding, and contrast ratios were chosen to ensure readability and usability for users with visual impairments.

4. Usability Testing Plan

Test Objectives:

  • Evaluate navigation efficiency
  • Assess medication input process
  • Verify reminder functionality clarity
  • Test appointment scheduling workflow
  • Evaluate health summary comprehension

Participant Criteria:

  • 8-10 participants aged 25-75
  • Mix of technology comfort levels
  • Currently managing multiple medications
  • Various chronic conditions represented

Test Tasks:

  • Add a new medication with custom schedule
  • Set up appointment reminders
  • Navigate between key screens

Data Collection Methods:

  • Think-aloud protocol
  • Task completion rates

  • Error rates
  • Post-test questionnaire
  • Recording of sessions (with permission)

5. Reflection

The UX design process for the MedTracker app has been instrumental in creating a solution that genuinely addresses user needs while highlighting the complexities of designing for diverse user groups. Through systematic research, persona development, journey mapping, and usability testing planning, I gained valuable insights into the challenges of medication management and healthcare coordination.

The secondary research approach proved particularly effective in understanding the broad spectrum of user needs. By analyzing the studies, I identified critical patterns in medication adherence challenges and technology comfort levels. This research-driven approach led to the development of two distinct personas - James and Margaret - who represent different ends of the user spectrum, from tech-savvy professionals to retired individuals with moderate technology comfort.

The creation of these personas helped with:

  • Highlighting the need for flexible reminder systems that work for both busy professionals and retirees
  • Identifying the importance of family access features for older users
  • Understanding the varying complexity levels needed in the interface
  • Recognizing the need for different data sharing capabilities based on user comfort

Challenges and Solutions

Several significant challenges emerged during the design process:

1. Balancing Complexity vs. Simplicity

Challenge: Creating an interface that served both tech-savvy users like James, who wanted advanced features, and users like Margaret, who preferred simplicity.

Solution: Implemented a sleek minimalist design that feels complete and offers all information a user may need without it being overwhelming.

2. Medication Schedule Complexity

Challenge: Accommodating various medication schedules, from simple daily doses to complex regimens with multiple conditions.

Solution: Developed a flexible scheduling system that can handle both basic and advanced timing requirements while maintaining a clear visual presentation.

3. Healthcare Provider Integration

Challenge: Creating a system that could facilitate communication between patients and multiple healthcare providers.

Solution: Implemented a structured health summary feature that can be easily shared with healthcare providers while maintaining user privacy.

4. Accessibility Considerations

Challenge: Ensuring the app was usable for older adults while maintaining its appeal to younger users.

Solution: Focused on clear typography, high contrast ratios, and intuitive navigation patterns that work well for all age groups.

This project has reinforced the importance of:

  • Thorough user research in informing design decisions
  • Creating inclusive designs that work for users with varying technical abilities
  • Balancing feature richness with usability
  • Incorporating feedback mechanisms throughout the design process
